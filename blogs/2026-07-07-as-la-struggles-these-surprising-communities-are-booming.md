Analysis from the Los Angeles Times found pockets of population growth in some surprising locations. Zev Yaroslavsky, a former L.A. city councilmember and county supervisor and the current director of the Los Angeles Initiative at the UCLA Luskin School of Public Affairs, said that overall, some of these areas had more room to grow than most urban areas in Los Angeles County.
