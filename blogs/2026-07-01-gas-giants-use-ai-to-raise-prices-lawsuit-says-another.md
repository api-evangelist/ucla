The Los Angeles Times reported on a new federal lawsuit filed by several California drivers accusing major gas chains of using AI software to collude and keep pump prices artificially high. The case tests California’s updated antitrust law, which now treats algorithm-driven common pricing as potential price-fixing, even if the companies are not directly in communication. “I wouldn’t be surprised if [this kind of individualized pricing] exists in all different kinds” of industries, said Robert Zeithammer, a professor at the UCLA Anderson School of Management.
